About us

History

School was founded in 2004 by Mrs. Kamla Jain. Mrs. Jain is now octogenarian lady. Her school is her life, for she loves children and is devoted to it. Mrs. Kamla Jain draws her lineage from her father ,Sh. Kundan Lal Ji, a noted educationalist and philanthropist who also founded Kundan Vidya Mandir, Ludhiana. She went to London to complete her P.G.C.E. and Academic Diploma in teaching. She did a certificate course in the Audio Visual Aids in Education. She acquired a British Scholarship to learn Dr. Rudolph Steiner’s methods in Education in Emerson Training College in East Grin Stead (Essex). A strict disciplinarian and a workaholic, she spends every spare instant of her endurance to the sanity of the education. Mrs. Kamla Jain’s Trust the promoter of the school is a “Not for Profit” Trust Committed to promote quality and value based education. Her diligent work and untiring efforts bore fruits and now the school has become a leading name in the education circle.

Message




Principal's Message

Learning is an unending process “I cannot teach anybody anything, I can only make them think”-Socrates. Open mindedness, a multicultural orientation, independence, a global outlook, multiple intelligences and abilities – these are the premium qualities needed today. As a 21st century organization, the Saraswati Modern sr. sec. school desires to set an approach to learning that incorporates inquiry, research, analytical thinking and an ethical approach that becomes a lifetime habit. In the process of developing various skills we stress most on thinking skills so that a child can introspect himself or her self analytically and become self dependent to face the challenges of outer world.




Founder's Message

Education is one of the greatest services provided by a teacher. It is vital for everything in life. The role played by teachers becomes a very important component as they are our nation builders. Education lays the foundation for installing values, attitudes and behaviors in a child. As a member of the teaching community, I am aware of the challenges confronted by students in the current scenario. Our young generation has a huge responsibility of making the right choices and taking right decisions as future citizens. As young administrators, engineers, scientist, doctors, executives in different capacities it becomes very imperative that they are not led astray into a blind alley. We, together with the parents, have to guide and lead them in such a way that they execute their duties in the best possible way. Only then can we build a new and vibrant India – India of Our Dream.
